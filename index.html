mcraig@g.harvard.edu — Curriculum Vitae

I am a first-year PhD student in Government and Social Policy, and a Graduate Fellow with the Center for American Political Studies. My research on race and ethnic politics in the U.S. focuses on intergroup contact, inequality, and education policy. I hold a BA in Political Science and Philosophy from Columbia University, and have completed a year of study in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics at the University of Oxford.

From Columbia University, I received a Human Rights Research Fellowship to study alternative peacebuilding practices in Civil Wars across the globe with Séverine Autesserre, contributing to the book project, The Frontlines of Peace (Oxford University Press, 2021). I also received a Laidlaw Research Fellowship to study undocumented immigration in the United States.

Prior to joining Harvard, I worked as a history teacher in Brooklyn K-12 schools and as a professional dancer with performance and choreography credits in Boston and New York City.

Research Interests

American Politics, Race and Ethnic Politics, Inequality, Intergroup Contact, Education Policy
